What we believe
The case for more homes in Glasgow

Glasgow has a housing crisis. The cause is a shortage of homes. The solution is building more of them. Here is the argument, step by step.

Step 1

Glasgow has a housing crisis

More than 6,000 households have live homelessness applications. Over 4,200 people are in temporary accommodation every night. Rents have risen 75% since 2010. This is not a normal housing market.

Read the evidence →
Step 2

Building more homes is the solution

Every city that has solved a housing crisis has done so by building significantly more homes. Public funding alone cannot fill the gap. Market rate housebuilding is the primary mechanism by which housing crises get resolved.

Read the argument →
Step 3

The rules make it too hard to build

Scotland's planning system, Section 75 agreements, energy standards, parking requirements, and affordable housing contributions all make building homes more expensive and more uncertain than it needs to be. The result is fewer homes built than Scotland needs.

Read about the barriers →
Step 4

What needs to change

Concrete demands for Glasgow City Council and the Scottish Government: faster planning, reformed Section 75, car-free development near transport, higher housing targets with teeth, and a Local Development Plan that treats housing as a priority.
