YIMBY evidence — cities that changed the rules
All five case studies

Five cities that changed their planning rules. Four success stories and one cautionary tale — which shows just as clearly that the policies work.

Auckland
New Zealand
Rents 28% lower
Upzoned 75% of residential land — 2016
In 2016 Auckland upzoned three-quarters of its residential land, allowing medium and high-density housing across the city. Construction records were broken. For the first time in a generation, housing supply outstripped population growth. Rents in Auckland rose 22% from 2017 to 2024, compared to 34% nationally and 36% in Wellington. Research estimates rents are now 28% lower than they would otherwise have been without the reform.
Greenaway-McGrevy & So (2024), University of Auckland. Read the research →
Lower Hutt
New Zealand
Rents down 21%
Citywide upzoning by a single council — from 2017
Lower Hutt (population 114,000) acted alone, upzoning 80% of residential land from 2017 without waiting for national reform. Construction tripled. Rents fell 21% relative to comparable cities that did not reform — proving a single council can act and see results.
Maltman & Greenaway-McGrevy (2024), Journal of Housing Economics. Read the research →
Minneapolis
USA
+1% vs +14% elsewhere
Eliminated single-family zoning citywide — 2020
Minneapolis became the first major US city to end single-family-only zoning, permitting duplexes and triplexes on all residential lots and removing parking minimums. From 2017 to 2022 it permitted 21,000 new units, vastly more than comparable Midwestern cities. Rents rose just 1% while the rest of Minnesota saw a 14% increase.
Pew Charitable Trusts (2023). Read the evidence →
Austin
USA
Rents fell
Zoning liberalisation and planning reform — from 2019
Austin overhauled its zoning codes, removed parking minimums, and streamlined permitting, making it far easier to build. It became one of the top US cities for apartment construction per capita. The result: rents fell while continuing to rise in most comparable American cities.
Pew Charitable Trusts / multiple sources. Read the evidence →
Croydon
London
4x homes — then reversed
Suburban Design Guidance — 2019 to 2022 (cautionary tale)
Croydon introduced Suburban Design Guidance in 2019 giving certainty to small-scale infill development. Net new homes in the borough quadrupled. A new mayor was then elected on a promise to scrap the policy. He did. Delivery immediately returned to pre-2019 levels. The clearest possible demonstration that YIMBY policies work — and that reversing them has an immediate and measurable cost.
Architects Journal (2026). Read the evidence →
