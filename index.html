What we believe
The YIMBY case for Glasgow
YIMBY — Yes In My Back Yard — is the belief that building more homes, in more places, is how cities solve housing crises. Here is the argument for Glasgow, step by step.
Step 1

Glasgow has a housing crisis

Rents up 75% since 2010. House prices up 70% since 2015. Glasgow is building only around 75% of its own housing target — itself likely far too low. When prices rise this fast and this persistently, the market is telling us something.

Read the evidence →
Step 2

Building more homes is the solution

Every city that has solved a housing crisis has done so by building significantly more homes. Public funding alone cannot fill the gap. Market-rate housebuilding is the primary mechanism by which housing crises get resolved.

Read the argument →
Step 3

The rules make it too hard to build

Scotland's planning system, Section 75 agreements, energy standards, parking requirements, and affordable housing contributions all make building homes more expensive and more uncertain than it needs to be.

Addressing the pushback
Common myths, real answers
Myth 1
"New builds push up house prices in the area."
The evidence consistently points the other way. More supply means lower prices — at city-wide and neighbourhood level.
Read the evidence →
Myth 2
"We should only build social housing."
Social housing is vital. But Scotland's funding gap means public money alone cannot solve the crisis. All types of housing help.
Read the evidence →
Myth 3
"Rent controls are the answer."
Controls protect existing tenants in the short run, but decades of evidence shows they reduce supply and raise rents over time.
Read the evidence →
